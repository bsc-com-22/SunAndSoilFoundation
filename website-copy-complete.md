### Who We Are

Sun & Soil Foundation is a Malawian non-profit organisation dedicated to transforming rural livelihoods through sustainable agriculture and clean energy solutions.

We believe the sun and the soil are Malawi's greatest assets. By harnessing solar energy for irrigation and promoting climate-smart farming practices, we help smallholder farmers increase yields, improve food security, and build long-term economic resilience.

Our work is grounded in community partnerships, local leadership, and practical solutions that respond directly to the realities farmers face on the ground.

---

### Why This Work Matters

**The Context**

Malawi's agricultural sector faces unprecedented challenges that threaten food security, rural livelihoods, and national development.

**The Reality**

- **80%** of Malawians depend on agriculture for survival
- **18.2%** face food insecurity annually (over 4 million people)
- **51%** of households remain below the poverty line

**Key Challenges**

- Erratic rainfall and prolonged droughts
- Rising costs of agricultural inputs and fuel
- Limited access to credit, technology, and markets
- Intensifying climate change impacts

**Our Solution**

- **Solar-powered irrigation** – Enabling year-round farming and reducing dependency on erratic rainfall patterns
- **Climate-smart techniques** – Improving yields sustainably through regenerative agricultural practices
- **Training and capacity building** – Empowering farmers and cooperatives with knowledge and skills for success
- **Community-led development** – Ensuring long-term impact through local ownership and leadership

### Our Story

**Born From a Vision of Self-Sufficiency**

Sun & Soil Foundation was established to address one of Malawi's most pressing challenges: the vulnerability of smallholder farmers to climate shocks and economic instability.

We recognised that Malawi's abundant sunshine and fertile soil could be leveraged to create year-round food production, reduce poverty, and build climate resilience. What began as a pilot solar irrigation project has grown into a comprehensive rural development programme reaching communities across the country.

Today, we work with 10 smallholder farmers directly and have trained 85 women, youth, and farmers in climate-smart agriculture and sustainable livelihoods. Our approach combines cutting-edge renewable energy technology with traditional agricultural knowledge, creating solutions that are both innovative and culturally appropriate.

By 2030, we aim to reach 50,000 farmers nationwide, establishing Sun & Soil Foundation as a leading force in Malawi's agricultural transformation.

### Introduction

At Sun & Soil Foundation, we deliver integrated solutions that address the root causes of food insecurity and rural poverty in Malawi. Our programmes combine renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, skills development, and enterprise support to create lasting change.

---

### Programme 1: Solar-Powered Irrigation

**Bringing Water to the Fields, Year-Round**

Malawi's agricultural calendar has traditionally been dictated by rainfall. But with climate change causing erratic weather patterns, farmers can no longer rely on the rains. Our solar-powered irrigation systems change that.

**What We Do**

- Install solar water pumping systems for individual farmers and cooperatives
- Provide drip irrigation kits for water-efficient farming
- Train farmers in irrigation management and system maintenance
- Support community-managed irrigation schemes

**Impact**

- Year-round crop production, even during dry seasons
- Multiple harvests per year, increasing household income
- Reduced dependency on expensive diesel-powered pumps
- Improved water use efficiency and soil health

**Who Benefits**

Smallholder farmers, women's groups, youth cooperatives, and community irrigation schemes across Malawi.

---

### Programme 2: Climate-Smart Agriculture

**Building Resilience Through Regenerative Farming**

Climate change is already affecting Malawi's farmers. We equip them with knowledge and techniques to adapt, thrive, and protect the environment.

**What We Do**

- Train farmers in soil conservation, crop rotation, and organic farming
- Promote drought-resistant and high-yield crop varieties
- Introduce agroforestry and intercropping systems
- Establish farmer field schools for peer-to-peer learning

**Impact**

- Improved soil fertility and water retention
- Higher crop yields with lower input costs
- Diversified income sources through mixed farming
- Reduced greenhouse gas emissions from agriculture

**Who Benefits**

Smallholder farmers, agricultural extension workers, and rural communities seeking sustainable livelihoods.

---

### Programme 3: Capacity Building & Mindset Change

**Empowering Farmers as Entrepreneurs**

Technical skills alone are not enough. We work to shift mindsets, build confidence, and develop the business acumen farmers need to succeed.

**What We Do**

- Deliver training in financial literacy, record-keeping, and business planning
- Facilitate savings groups and cooperative formation
- Provide mentorship and ongoing technical support
- Promote gender equality and youth leadership in agriculture

**Impact**

- Farmers transition from subsistence to commercial agriculture
- Increased access to credit and markets
- Stronger farmer organisations and cooperatives
- Greater participation of women and youth in decision-making

**Who Benefits**

Farmers, cooperatives, women's groups, and young agripreneurs.

---

### Programme 4: Women & Youth Enterprise Development

**Creating Opportunities for the Next Generation**

Women and youth are often excluded from agricultural value chains. We change that by providing targeted support for enterprise development.

**What We Do**

- Offer startup capital and equipment for agribusinesses
- Provide training in value addition, marketing, and sales
- Connect entrepreneurs to markets and buyers
- Support women and youth cooperatives

**Impact**

- New income-generating enterprises in rural areas
- Increased economic participation of women and youth
- Job creation and skills development
- Reduced rural-urban migration

**Who Benefits**

Women farmers, young entrepreneurs, school leavers, and rural cooperatives.

---

### Programme 5: Sustainable Financing & Reinvestment

**Making Impact Self-Sustaining**

We believe in creating systems that outlast donor funding. Our sustainable financing model ensures long-term impact.

**What We Do**

- Facilitate access to microfinance and agricultural loans
- Establish revolving funds for equipment and inputs
- Link farmers to savings and credit cooperatives (SACCOs)
- Promote income reinvestment for farm expansion

**Impact**

- Farmers access affordable credit without exploitative interest rates
- Communities build financial resilience
- Programmes become self-sustaining over time
- Reduced dependency on external aid

**Who Benefits**

Farmers, cooperatives, and community-based organisations.

---

### Our Approach

**Community-Led, Evidence-Based, Scalable**

We don't impose solutions. We co-create them with the communities we serve.

**1. Community Engagement**
We start by listening. Our programmes are designed with input from farmers, local leaders, and community members.

**2. Pilot & Learn**
We test new approaches on a small scale, measure results, and refine before scaling.

**3. Capacity Building**
We invest in training and mentorship to ensure communities can sustain programmes independently.

**4. Partnerships**
We collaborate with government, NGOs, private sector, and research institutions to maximise impact.

**5. Monitoring & Evaluation**
We track progress rigorously and share learnings transparently.

### Stories From the Field

**Real People. Real Change.**

**Grace's Story: From One Harvest to Three**

Grace, a smallholder farmer in Salima, used to harvest maize once a year during the rainy season. After receiving a solar irrigation system and training from Sun & Soil Foundation, she now grows vegetables year-round. Her income has tripled, and she's sending her children to secondary school.

**The Dedza Youth Cooperative**

A group of young farmers in Dedza formed a cooperative with our support. They now manage a solar-powered irrigation scheme, grow high-value crops, and supply local markets. What started with 8 members has grown to 25, creating jobs and keeping youth in rural areas.

**Transforming a Community**

In one village, our farmer field school brought together 40 farmers to learn climate-smart techniques. Today, the entire community practices crop rotation, composting, and water conservation. Yields have increased by 40%, and the village has become a model for neighbouring communities.
