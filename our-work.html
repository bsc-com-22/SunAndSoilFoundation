Our Work

Transforming rural livelihoods through solar energy, climate-smart agriculture, and community empowerment.

OUR APPROACH

Integrated Solutions for Lasting Change

At Sun & Soil Foundation, we deliver integrated solutions that address the root causes of food insecurity and rural poverty in Malawi. Our programmes combine renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, skills development, and enterprise support to create lasting change.

We don't impose solutions. We co-create them with the communities we serve, ensuring that every intervention is locally owned, culturally appropriate, and built to last.

PROGRAMME 1

Solar-Powered Irrigation

Bringing water to the fields, year-round. Malawi's agricultural calendar has traditionally been dictated by rainfall. But with climate change causing erratic weather patterns, farmers can no longer rely on the rains. Our solar-powered irrigation systems change that.

What We Do

Install solar water pumping systems for individual farmers and cooperatives. Provide drip irrigation kits for water-efficient farming. Train farmers in irrigation management and system maintenance. Support community-managed irrigation schemes.

Impact

Year-round crop production, even during dry seasons. Multiple harvests per year, increasing household income. Reduced dependency on expensive diesel-powered pumps. Improved water use efficiency and soil health.

Who Benefits

Smallholder farmers, women's groups, youth cooperatives, and community irrigation schemes across Malawi.

PROGRAMME 2

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Building resilience through regenerative farming. Climate change is already affecting Malawi's farmers. We equip them with knowledge and techniques to adapt, thrive, and protect the environment.

What We Do

Train farmers in soil conservation, crop rotation, and organic farming. Promote drought-resistant and high-yield crop varieties. Introduce agroforestry and intercropping systems. Establish farmer field schools for peer-to-peer learning.

Impact

Improved soil fertility and water retention. Higher crop yields with lower input costs. Diversified income sources through mixed farming. Reduced greenhouse gas emissions from agriculture.

Who Benefits

Smallholder farmers, agricultural extension workers, and rural communities seeking sustainable livelihoods.

PROGRAMME 3

Capacity Building & Mindset Change

Empowering farmers as entrepreneurs. Technical skills alone are not enough. We work to shift mindsets, build confidence, and develop the business acumen farmers need to succeed.

Training & Development

Deliver training in financial literacy, record-keeping, and business planning. Facilitate savings groups and cooperative formation. Provide mentorship and ongoing technical support.

Transformation

Farmers transition from subsistence to commercial agriculture. Increased access to credit and markets. Stronger farmer organisations and cooperatives.

Inclusion

Greater participation of women and youth in decision-making. Gender equality and youth leadership in agriculture promoted throughout.

PROGRAMME 4

Women & Youth Enterprise Development

Creating opportunities for the next generation. Women and youth are often excluded from agricultural value chains. We change that by providing targeted support for enterprise development.

Startup Support

Offer startup capital and equipment for agribusinesses. Provide training in value addition, marketing, and sales. Connect entrepreneurs to markets and buyers.

Economic Impact

New income-generating enterprises in rural areas. Increased economic participation of women and youth. Job creation and skills development.

Community Benefit

Reduced rural-urban migration. Women farmers and young entrepreneurs empowered. Rural cooperatives strengthened and sustained.

PROGRAMME 5

Sustainable Financing & Reinvestment

Making impact self-sustaining. We believe in creating systems that outlast donor funding. Our sustainable financing model ensures long-term impact.

Access to Finance

Facilitate access to microfinance and agricultural loans. Establish revolving funds for equipment and inputs. Link farmers to savings and credit cooperatives (SACCOs).

Sustainability

Farmers access affordable credit without exploitative interest rates. Communities build financial resilience. Programmes become self-sustaining over time.

Independence

Reduced dependency on external aid. Income reinvestment for farm expansion promoted. Long-term financial independence achieved.

OUR METHODOLOGY

Community-Led, Evidence-Based, Scalable

We don't impose solutions. We co-create them with the communities we serve.

Community Engagement

We start by listening. Our programmes are designed with input from farmers, local leaders, and community members.

Pilot & Learn

We test new approaches on a small scale, measure results, and refine before scaling.

Capacity Building

We invest in training and mentorship to ensure communities can sustain programmes independently.

Partnerships

We collaborate with government, NGOs, private sector, and research institutions to maximise impact.

Monitoring & Evaluation

We track progress rigorously and share learnings transparently with all stakeholders.
